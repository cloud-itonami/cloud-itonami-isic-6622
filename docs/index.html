ISIC Rev.5: 6622

Activities of insurance agents and brokers

An open-source business blueprint for licensed operators

The problem: LLMs excel at drafting customer needs and comparing insurance quotes—but they have no notion of jurisdiction producer-licensing requirements, no way to verify if a broker has an undisclosed conflict of interest with the highest-paying insurer, and no authority to bind coverage. Deploying an LLM directly exposes you to fabricated licensing citations, steering customers toward commissions instead of customer needs, and silent liability.

The solution: An open, forkable business blueprint and runtime that seals the Broker-LLM into a single node and wraps it with an independent Insurance Intermediation Governor. The Governor enforces four hard compliance checks (spec-basis citations, conflict-of-interest screening, minimum quote comparison, jurisdiction commission caps) and mandates human approval on all high-stakes decisions—placement binding and commission booking never run autonomous, at any phase, by construction.

How it stops unsafe commits

  • Spec-basis check: every placement requires a cited official jurisdiction source (not LLM guesses)
  • Conflict-of-interest screening: undisclosed broker relationships are hard-hold violations that cannot be approved
  • Minimum quote validation: placements must compare ≥2 insurers to prove best-interest advice
  • Commission-cap enforcement: jurisdiction rate limits are un-overridable by human operator
  • Immutable audit ledger: every intake, assessment, screening, binding, and booking decision is recorded and append-only
What this is NOT: This actor does not claim to hold a license to place or bind insurance in any jurisdiction. Whoever deploys and operates a live instance (a licensed independent agent or brokerage) supplies the jurisdiction-specific license, real carrier relationships, and placement/commission processing integrations, and bears that jurisdiction's liability. The software supplies the governed, spec-cited, audited execution scaffold so that operator does not build the compliance layer from scratch.
